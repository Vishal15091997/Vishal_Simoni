Mangalgarh - The Fort of Auspiciousness

A Journey Through Time and Royalty

Welcome to Fort Mangal Garh Palace, Charkhari — a timeless heritage palace hotel reflecting the royal legacy of Maharaja Chhatrashal and the era of Bajirao–Mastani. Once the seat of the princely state of Charkhari, it later became Uttar Pradesh’s first original heritage palace transformed into a luxury hotel.

Within the historic palace of Maharaja Chhatrasal, the eternal love story of Bajirao–Mastani lives on, expressed through magnificent Bundeli folk performances and enriched by traditional royal feasts. At Fort Mangal Garh Palace, embark on a timeless journey where heritage, romance, and regal hospitality unite.

Over the generations, it has been the residence of royals, witnessing tales of bravery, grand celebrations, and cultural richness. Every courtyard, corridor, and carved pillar echoes with the whispers of a bygone era.

Located in the historic town of Charkhari, famous for its 108 Krishna temples and seven serene lakes, the palace offers stunning views of lush gardens and the majestic Mangal Garh Fort overlooking the city.

Architectural Magnificence

The palace is a breathtaking symphony of traditional craftsmanship and regal aesthetics. Featuring sprawling lush courtyards, beautifully sculpted pillars, and ornate domes, it reflects the pinnacle of architectural brilliance. Every intricate carving and grand hall tells a story of elegance, providing an ethereal and deeply romantic setting that perfectly bridges historical authenticity with modern celebration.

Chapter 1

Where History Meets the "Kashmir of Bundelkhand"

Perched majestically in the heart of Charkhari—a town celebrated as the "Kashmir of Bundelkhand" and "Mini Vrindavan"—Fort Mangalgarh Palace stands as a timeless sentinel of Rajput grandeur. Surrounded by serene water bodies and ancient temples, our palace is more than a hotel; it is a living chronicle of royal valor, architectural brilliance, and an enduring symbol of love.

1734

A Foundation Laid in Fortune

The saga of Mangalgarh began in 1734 when Jagat Raj, the illustrious son of the legendary Maharaja Chhatrasal, chose Ranjit Hill as the site for his stronghold. Legend tells of a successful hunt for the chakhar deer that led the King to this spot. Because the foundation was laid on a Mangalwar (Tuesday), the fort was christened Mangalgarh—the Fort of Auspiciousness.

1885–1895

The Golden Era of Architecture

While the foundations were ancient, the palace reached its architectural zenith under the reign of Maharaja Malkhan Singh (the grandson of Maharaja Chhatrasal). Between 1885 and 1895, the structure was expanded into the palatial marvel seen today. Through the centuries, the halls of Mangalgarh have been graced by a lineage of noble rulers, including Maharaja Jujhar Singh, Maharaja Ganga Singh, Maharaja Arimardan Singh, and Maharaja Jayendra Singh.

Chapter 4

The Land of Eternal Love

Fort Mangalgarh is not just a military bastion it is a site of deep cultural and historical significance. Fort Mangalgarh holds a unique place in the heart of Indian history as the sacred ground where the historic union of Peshwa Bajirao and Mastani was solemnized. This legacy of royal romance permeates the palace, as the palace has been restored to welcome guests seeking a blend of timeless charm and modern luxury, offering guests a rare opportunity to walk the same corridors where one of history's greatest love stories was written.

The New Era

A Visionary Restoration

Once a defensive stronghold, the palace has been transformed into a boutique heritage hotel that preserves its grand walls and vibrant Bundelkhandi culture. After India's independence, it remained with royal descendants Shree Jayant Singh and Shree Jayveer Singh, before its preservation was entrusted to Shri Ganga Charan Rajput. Driven by his vision to revive the glory of Charkhari State, he restored Fort Mangalgarh to share the timeless charm of the "Kashmir of Bundelkhand" with the world.

Today

Experience Royal Hospitality

Today, Fort Mangalgarh Palace has been thoughtfully converted into a boutique heritage hotel. Every effort has been made to preserve the original Bundelkhandi craftsmanship while integrating modern luxuries. We invite you to step through our gates, where the echoes of 250 years of history meet the warmth of traditional hospitality. Come, stay where kings once resided, and become a part of the continuing legacy of Mangalgarh.
